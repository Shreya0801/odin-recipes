Chole Bhature

Home

Chole Bhature is a popular North Indian dish made of spicy chickpeas (chole) served with deep-fried fluffy bread (bhature). It's a beloved street food and comfort meal, especially enjoyed for breakfast or lunch.

The chole is cooked with onions, tomatoes, and aromatic Indian spices to create a rich and flavorful curry. The bhature, made from fermented dough, is crispy on the outside and soft on the inside, making it a perfect pairing with the spicy chickpeas. This hearty dish is often accompanied by sliced onions, pickles, and a glass of lassi.

Ingredients

For Chole Preparation

  • Overnight soaked Chickpeas
  • Onions
  • Tomatoes
  • Ginger
  • Garlic
  • Green Chillies
  • Cumin seeds
  • Bay leaf
  • Cinnamon
  • Black Cardamom
  • Turmeric Powder
  • Red Chilli Powder
  • Green Cardamom
  • Black Pepper
  • Cooking oil(Mustard oil)
  • Salt
  • Garam Masala

For Bhatura Preparation

  • Maida
  • sugar
  • salt
  • water
  • curd
  • baking soda
  • oil

Steps for making:

For Chole

  1. Pressure cook the soaked chickpeas with salt and water until soft (usually 4-5 whistles).
  2. Heat oil in a pan, add cumin seeds, bay leaf, cinnamon, and cardamom.
  3. Add finely chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  4. Add ginger-garlic paste and chopped green chillies. Sauté for a minute.
  5. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until soft and the oil starts to separate.
  6. Add turmeric, red chilli powder, and salt. Mix well.
  7. Add the boiled chickpeas and a bit of the water they were cooked in.
  8. Add garam masala and simmer for 10-15 minutes so the flavors blend.
  9. Mash a few chickpeas to thicken the gravy and adjust salt/spice as needed.

For Bhature

  1. In a large bowl, mix maida, salt, sugar, baking soda, and curd.
  2. Gradually add water and knead into a soft, smooth dough.
  3. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 2–3 hours.
  4. After resting, divide the dough into small balls.
  5. Roll each ball into a circular shape like a poori (not too thin).
  6. Heat oil in a deep pan and fry each bhatura until puffed and golden on both sides.
  7. Remove and place on paper towels to absorb extra oil.
