Aloo Paratha

Home

Aloo Paratha is a popular Indian stuffed flatbread made with whole wheat dough filled with a spiced mashed potato mixture. It's a favorite breakfast dish in North India but can be enjoyed at any time of the day.

The paratha is cooked on a hot griddle with ghee or oil until golden and crispy on both sides. It’s typically served hot with butter, curd (yogurt), or pickle for a flavorful and filling meal.

Ingredients required:

For Stuffing

  • Boiled Potatoes
  • Green Chilli
  • Black Pepper
  • Garlic
  • Coriander Leaves
  • cumin seeds
  • salt
  • oil

For Dough

  • Whole Wheat flour
  • Water
  • salt

Steps for making Aloo Paratha

For Stuffing:

  1. Mash the boiled potatoes in a bowl.
  2. Heat a small amount of oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  3. Add chopped garlic and green chillies. Sauté for a few seconds.
  4. Pour this tadka (tempering) over the mashed potatoes.
  5. Add salt, black pepper, and chopped coriander leaves.
  6. Mix everything well to form the stuffing.

For Dough:

  1. In a bowl, mix whole wheat flour and salt.
  2. Gradually add water and knead it into a smooth dough.
  3. Cover and rest the dough for 15-20 minutes.

Assembling and Cooking:

  1. Divide the dough into small balls.
  2. Roll out one ball into a small circle.
  3. Place a spoonful of stuffing in the center.
  4. Bring the edges together to seal it, then gently flatten and roll it out again.
  5. Heat a tawa or pan, and cook the paratha on both sides using a little oil or ghee.
  6. Cook until golden brown spots appear and both sides are well-roasted.
