Dal Fara

Fara prepared from rice and lentils is a tasty and healthy dish. It is widely consumed in Uttar Pradesh. Fara is eaten and prepared in almost the entire state with some variation. Not much spices and oil are used in preparing Fara. You can prepare and eat it for breakfast or lunch. Let us know the method of preparing Fara.

Ingridents required

  • Rice flour
  • Urad Dal
  • Chana Dal
  • Green Chillies
  • Garlic Paste
  • Red Chilli powder
  • Turmeric powder
  • Cumin seeds
  • Mstard Seeds(Rai)
  • Asafoetida
  • salt
  • Ghee
  • Coriander powder
  • Coriander leaves

Steps for Preparation

  1. To make fara, take a pan and add 1 cup water, 2 tablespoons ghee, and ½ tablespoon salt. Bring it to a boil.
  2. Once boiled, remove it from heat and add rice flour to the water. Mix well and cover it for a few minutes.
  3. Soak chana dal (split chickpeas) and urad dal (split black gram) overnight or for 4-5 hours. Drain the water and grind them coarsely.
  4. To the ground dal mixture, add chopped green chillies, ginger paste, red chilli powder, turmeric, coriander powder, fresh coriander leaves, and salt. Mix well — this is the stuffing for the fara.
  5. Now, knead the rice dough with a little ghee to make it smooth. Add a little water if needed.
  6. Divide the dough into small portions and roll them out like a small poori (flat circle). If the edges crack, cut them neatly with a round lid or bowl.
  7. Place some dal stuffing in the center and fold the circle in half. Seal the edges by pressing gently.
  8. Prepare all fara this way. Boil water in a deep pan and place the fara in a steamer above the boiling water. Cover and steam on low flame until cooked.
  9. Once ready, serve the steamed fara with chutney, or slice them and sauté in ghee with cumin seeds, mustard seeds, and green chillies for added flavor.
